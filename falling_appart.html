Falling Apart is a third person platformer that make use of known classic game codes to trick the player into getting involved with its narrative.

PC

Gameplay

The player use many movement abilities to explore and progress through a 3D environment. The goal of the game is to understand each levels and complete them by collecting all objectives scattered in them.

Control scheme

The game’s controls were built over a classic third person shooter control scheme.

The player can move, look around, and jump over obstacles.

Sprint

Upon entering it, the zone temporary absorb the player’s momentum, holding him in suspension. The player can then exit the zone by performing a dash whose strength depends of the previously accumulated momentum.

He can use this momentum to move faster and close bigger gaps with his jump.

Stasis Zone

The player’s second main ability is the stasis zone. It’s a small, sphere shaped, zone that the player can deploy in an area around him by shooting.

It’s big enough so the player can get inside.

Upon entering it, the zone temporary absorb the player’s momentum, holding him in suspension.

The player can then exit the zone by performing a dash whose strength depends of the previously accumulated momentum.

Uses examples :

  • Access various areas by using the zone as a platform.
  • Close bigger gaps by chaining jump and subsequent zone entrances and dashes exits.
  • Converting horizontal momentum to vertical momentum.
  • Prevent fall damage by using the zone as a cushion.
Narrative

The game’s divided in various levels, each of them inspired by the several stages of grief.

The player is lead to come across many new and interesting things. He will first create a relationship with those. But, in the end, he’ll have to let go of every one of them and focus on building up his own strength.

Denial

In this first level, the player is introduced to the game’s mechanics. The games encourages him to play with those, in what seems to be a playground environment.

Although, the player won't progress until he understands there is a greater goal to strive for, and use the mechanics he learned to achieve it.

Anger

During this step, the player is introduced with a new mechanic he can use to destroy the environment. Its input is fun to execute, and its consequences are enjoyable to watch.

That is until he realises that those firework like explosion he creates aren't doing any good.

Sadness

The player is led into a torrent that is easy to sweep along. The more the player sinks down the stream, the more gloomy the atmosphere becomes.

The player have to turn against the flow of water and make use of everything he learned to face it and progress through the most difficult part of the game.

Acceptance

This is the grand final of the game. The player have mastered all the mechanics of the game.

He is presented to the ghost figure of the main character’s lost dear one. As the player try to catch up the ghost, the gap increases. No matter how energy he puts in, how well he performs, in the end, he’ll never be able to catch up.

He finally have to chose to go a separate path, maybe looking over his shoulder to see the ghost waving him goodbye.
